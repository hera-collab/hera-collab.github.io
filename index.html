Welcome to the
HERA Collaboration

The High-altitude Engineering for Research in Astrophysics (HERA) Collaboration is an international collaboration to engage students in astrophysics research through particle physics experiments on the ground and on high-altitude balloons. This program focuses on advancing STEM education and mentoring while contributing to astrophysical research. Each participating team, consisting of a university and high school, launches high-altitude balloons equipped with muon detectors and other team-selected experiments.

HERA is currently in the pilot phase and is primarily comprised of teams from the United States and Australia. We are conducting coordinated launches, developing custom payloads, and working to expand the program. If your institution is interested in joining HERA, please contact Alissa Sperling, Christy Love, or Jackie Bondell.

Project Goals

HERA aims to investigate interactions between cosmic rays and the Earth's atmosphere. The initial phase of the project involves a coordinated, multinational high-altitude balloon launch to study particle flux with respect to latitude, altitude, magnetic field, and local weather conditions.

STEM Education &
Skill Development

  1. Engage students in meaningful scientific inquiry and engineering challenges by involving them in the entire process, from designing experiments to launching and recovering balloons.
  2. Provide students with hands-on learning opportunities in fields such as astrophysics, engineering, atmospheric science, and data analysis.
  3. Cultivate problem-solving, teamwork, and technical skills that will prepare students for future careers in STEM.

Mentorship & Collaboration

  1. Strengthen mentorship connections between high school students and faculty and university students and faculty, fostering a supportive learning environment.
  2. Create sustainable local institutional partnerships that encourage resource-sharing and collaboration between secondary and higher education institutions.
  3. Encourage collaboration across teams and institutions, both locally and globally, to create a community of learners contributing to scientific discovery.
  4. Provide students exposure to academic pathways in astrophysics, engineering, and other STEM fields through active participation in research.
